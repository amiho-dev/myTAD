## If Your Account Has Been Banned

### What Does This Mean?
Your account and device have been restricted from accessing myTAD. This typically means:
- Your account violated our Terms of Service
- Your device was associated with rule-breaking activity
- An admin has applied this restriction

### How Device Bans Work
The system identifies you by:
- Your **IP Address**
- Your **Device Fingerprint** (browser + operating system characteristics)

This means:
- **You cannot login** from your current device
- **You cannot register** a new account from your current device
- **You cannot bypass** this by:
  - Clearing cookies/cache
  - Using incognito mode
  - Using a different browser
  - Using different email addresses

### Why Can't I Just Create a New Account?
Creating a new account from a banned device will:
1. Immediately ban that new account too
2. Make all your accounts permanently banned
3. Permanently block your device from myTAD

**Don't attempt this.** The warning is real.

## Appealing a Ban

### Permanent Ban Appeal Process
If you believe your ban was unfair:

1. **Document your case**: 
   - Why you believe the ban was a mistake
   - Any mitigating circumstances
   - Any evidence supporting your case

2. **Contact support**:
   - Email: [support email]
   - Include:
     - Your banned username
     - Brief explanation
     - Your evidence

3. **Appeal review**:
   - Admins will review your case
   - Response typically within 5-7 business days
   - You'll receive a decision

4. **If approved**:
   - Your account and device bans are lifted
   - You can login normally
   - You may receive a warning about future conduct

### Temporary Ban Appeal
If you believe your temporary ban was in error:
- You can appeal, but waiting out the ban may be faster
- If ban is short-term (< 24 hours), it's usually faster to just wait

## Preventing Future Bans

### Follow the Rules
- Read and understand the Terms of Service
- Don't engage in:
  - Cheating or exploiting glitches
  - Harassment or abuse
  - Account sharing (except family devices)
  - Spam or botting
  - Hacking attempts

### Be Honest with Support
- If you accidentally broke a rule, admit it
- This shows good faith for future appeals
- First-time violations often get mercy

### Protect Your Account
- Use a strong, unique password
- Enable two-factor authentication
- Don't share your login credentials
- Report suspicious account activity

## Different Devices & Accounts

### If You Share a Device
Family or workplace devices may have multiple accounts:
- If one account is banned, others on **same IP** may also be restricted
- Contact support to explain device sharing
- Admins can sometimes whitelist shared devices

### If You Use Multiple Devices
Different devices = different fingerprints and IPs:
- Ban affects only the device it was applied to
- You can use other devices (unless IP is shared)
- Contact support if all devices are blocked

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q: Can I use a VPN to bypass my ban?
**A:** No. VPNs:
- Change your IP but not your device fingerprint
- May actually make things worse
- Could result in permanent ban
- Are monitored by admins

### Q: What if I'm at a library/cafe and it's IP-banned?
**A:** Contact support. Explain:
- You're using a public network
- Your device isn't personally banned
- Your account might be unbanned for different IPs
- Provide your device details for verification

### Q: Can I appeal a ban by creating a new account?
**A:** Absolutely not. This will:
- Get that new account banned too
- Make all accounts permanently banned
- Worsen your situation
- Violate Terms of Service again

### Q: How long does an appeal take?
**A:** Typically 5-7 business days
- Complex cases may take longer
- You'll receive status updates
- Some appeals are reviewed faster if clear

### Q: Will I lose my progress/items/data?
**A:** It depends on the ban policy:
- Some bans = data preserved until appeal approved
- Some bans = data reset after X days
- Check the specific Terms of Service

### Q: Can I talk to an admin directly?
**A:** Contact support. They'll:
- Review your case
- Forward to appropriate admin if needed
- Keep you informed of progress
